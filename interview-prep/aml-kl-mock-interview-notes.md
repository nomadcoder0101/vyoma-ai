## Investigation Framework

When explaining how to investigate a transaction monitoring alert:

1. Review the triggering scenario and understand why the alert was generated.
2. Analyse customer profile, risk rating, occupation, expected behaviour, and historical activity.
3. Review counterparties, geography, transaction velocity, linked accounts, and fund flow.
4. Identify typologies such as structuring, layering, rapid movement of funds, mule activity, or high-risk jurisdiction exposure.
5. Validate source of funds, source of wealth, adverse media, and sanctions exposure where required.
6. Decide whether to close with documented rationale or escalate for SAR/STR/SMR consideration.

Useful phrases:

- Potential structuring behaviour
- Contextual review
- Aggregation over time
- Economic rationale
- Unusual but expected vs suspicious
- Reasonable grounds for suspicion based on cumulative indicators

## Malaysia / BNM / AMLA Refresh

Key points:

- Bank Negara Malaysia supervises AML/CFT compliance for financial institutions.
- AMLA requires risk-based customer due diligence, ongoing monitoring, STR reporting, internal controls, record keeping, training, audit, and governance.
- PEPs require enhanced due diligence, senior management approval, source of wealth/source of funds review, enhanced ongoing monitoring, and more frequent review.
- Beneficial ownership means the natural person who ultimately owns or controls the customer or on whose behalf a transaction is conducted.
- If no beneficial owner is identified through ownership or control after reasonable measures, identify the senior managing official according to policy.
- AMLA aligns with FATF risk-based principles, which supports transferability from AUSTRAC experience to Malaysia.

## Source of Funds vs Source of Wealth

Interview-ready answer:

> Source of Funds refers to the origin of money used in a specific transaction or account activity, while Source of Wealth refers to how a customer accumulated their overall wealth over time. Source of Funds helps validate individual transactions, whereas Source of Wealth supports broader risk assessment, particularly during Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk customers.

Example:

- SoF: RM1 million came from a property sale last month.
- SoW: Customer accumulated wealth through 20 years of running a manufacturing business.

## Sanctions Hit Workflow

Avoid saying the analyst personally blocks, freezes, reports, or closes the account.

Correct workflow:

1. Review the alert, list, match score, customer profile, and risk rating.
2. Compare identifiers: full name, date of birth, nationality, address, passport or national ID.
3. Decide whether it is a false positive, true match, or inconclusive match.
4. Document false positives with rationale.
5. Escalate potential true hits to Compliance/Sanctions according to policy.
6. Maintain auditable documentation in the case management system.

## L1 / L2 / L3 Transaction Monitoring

- L1: Alert triage, initial screening, customer profile review, false positive closure, escalation of genuine concerns.
- L2: Deep investigation, fund flow tracing, beneficiary linkages, KYC refresh, adverse media checks, typology assessment, investigation narrative.
- L3: Compliance review, validation of suspicious activity, STR filing, legal/regulatory coordination where required.
